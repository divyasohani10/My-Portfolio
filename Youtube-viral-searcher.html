Overview

The Objective

This system helps creators and marketers find YouTube content ideas that are already proven to work. By searching any keyword, scoring every video using a custom virality formula, filtering out low-quality results, and saving ranked data to Google Sheets — it turns hours of manual research into a 30-second automated process.

The Challenge

Scoring Virality Accurately

Raw view counts are misleading — a video with 1 million views from a 10 million subscriber channel is far less viral than one with 500K views from a 5K subscriber channel. The challenge was building a scoring model that correctly weights performance relative to channel size, while also filtering duplicates as the dataset grows over time.

The Solution

Technical Strategy

n8n triggers via a form where users enter a keyword, time window, and result count. Apify scrapes YouTube data and returns views, likes, subscriber counts, titles, and thumbnails. Each video is scored using the formula: score = views / (subscribers^(2/3)) / 10 — which surfaces overperforming content regardless of channel size. Results are sorted by score, checked against existing Google Sheets entries to remove duplicates, and only unique high-scoring videos are saved.

Key Results

Impact

  • Hours of manual research replaced by 30-second automation
  • Custom virality score surfaces hidden underdog viral content
  • Duplicate prevention keeps the dataset clean over time
  • Structured output ready for content strategy decisions
